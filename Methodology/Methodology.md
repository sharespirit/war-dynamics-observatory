# Methodology

The War Dynamics Observatory evaluates conflicts using a structured analytical model designed to reduce narrative bias and improve transparency.

Instead of relying on political narratives, the Observatory evaluates conflicts through **observable dynamics** measured across fifteen indicators.

Each indicator is evaluated using three parameters:

Score  
Relevance  
Confidence


---

## Score

Score measures the **current intensity or strength** of the phenomenon being evaluated.

Scale: **1–10**

Higher values indicate stronger or more extreme conditions.


---

## Relevance

Relevance measures **how important the indicator is in the specific conflict**.

Not all indicators are equally decisive in every conflict.

Scale: **1–5**


---

## Confidence

Confidence measures **how reliable the evaluation is given the available evidence**.

Conflict environments often involve incomplete or uncertain data.

Scale: **1–5**


---

# Indicator Structure

The analytical framework evaluates conflicts through **15 indicators grouped into three domains**.


---

# Conflict Dynamics

These indicators measure the **internal dynamics of the war itself**.

War Intensity  
Operational Momentum  
Escalation Risk  
War Sustainability  
Strategic Initiative


---

# Strategic Balance

These indicators measure the **structural balance between the actors involved**.

Military Balance  
Economic Endurance  
Energy Security  
Industrial Capacity  
Technological Edge


---

# Systemic Context

These indicators measure the **broader environment shaping the conflict**.

Political Stability  
Social Resilience  
Diplomatic Position  
Information Dominance  
Geographic Advantage


---

# Analytical Principle

All indicators are evaluated simultaneously.

The goal is not to predict the future, but to make the **current dynamics of the conflict observable and transparent**.

By combining structured indicators with explicit reasoning, the War Dynamics Observatory aims to improve the quality of public debate about war and international security.
